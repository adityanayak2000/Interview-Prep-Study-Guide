## Process Overview
Public India campus OA packs are thin; most detailed write-ups are Taiwan (Hsinchu) campus/new-grad loops: multi-department first interviews → second round → offer. Software/system tracks emphasize whiteboard/linked-list coding, C efficiency, and OS/RTOS concepts. Bengaluru FAE and partner hiring also appear — treat process as **department-dependent**.

## Online Assessment (OA)
- **Platform:** Not consistently documented for India campus; Taiwan loops often skip a shared OA and go straight to department interviews.
- **Duration / sections:** N/A for a standardized India OA pack.
- **Topics tested (interviews):** Linked lists, bit operations, C performance (`const`, algorithms), OS/RTOS (interrupts, mutex/semaphore), role-specific (Wi-Fi, SSD, Ethernet).
- **Difficulty:** Medium–Hard on fundamentals + live coding.

## Interview Rounds
- **Dept interviews (often many in “一面”):** Self-intro → resume → whiteboard/paper coding (LL heavily reported) → personality.
- **二面:** Deeper dept fit; bitwise common in storage/SSD-adjacent panels.
- **Offer:** Verbal → written; timeline can span weeks.

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- Drill linked lists cold (reverse, cycle, merge) — repeatedly cited across Realtek software interviews.
- Know why your C is efficient; interviewers may push “coding method” over compiler magic.
- For India: confirm JD (FAE vs firmware vs SW) before assuming Taiwan OA pattern.
