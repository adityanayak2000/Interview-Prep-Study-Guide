## Process Overview
On-campus India drives commonly use a CoCubes (or similar) online assessment, then two technical interviews and HR. Round count varies (about 2–5 total after OA). Software tracks stress OS concurrency, endianness/bits, and linked-list/stack DSA more than product-company Hard algorithms.

## Online Assessment (OA)
- **Platform:** CoCubes frequently reported for campus; duration often ~65–90 minutes.
- **Sections & format:** MCQ-heavy — aptitude, C fundamentals (pointers), OS, Computer Organization & Architecture, tree-traversal MCQs; some papers ~30 objective + coding, others ~49 objectives across aptitude / C / electronics / profile electives.
- **Coding (when present):** 1–2 C/C++ problems — clone linked list, reverse list in groups of K, tree diameter.
- **Negative marking:** Not consistently documented; treat time pressure as the main filter.
- **Topics tested:** C pointers, OS, CAO, trees theory, light aptitude; electronics for hardware-inclusive papers.
- **Difficulty:** Medium.

## Interview Rounds
- **Technical Round 1:** OS scheduling comparisons; producer–consumer (pseudocode); endianness; bitwise (missing number / XOR tricks); string reverse / word reverse.
- **Technical Round 2:** Resume/project deep-dive; light design (e.g. multi-user Bluetooth bulb scenario); power function optimized with bits; OS types/use cases.
- **HR:** Why MediaTek, strengths/weaknesses, culture/expectations, feedback on prior rounds.

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- Practice **maximum linked-list and stack** questions — repeatedly cited by candidates.
- OS scheduling + producer–consumer is near-guaranteed oral material.
- Endianness and bit tricks bridge software and silicon talk — rehearse aloud.
- Process can move quickly after OA; know MediaTek’s product positioning for HR.
