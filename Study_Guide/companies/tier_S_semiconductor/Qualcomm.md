## Process Overview
Campus and off-campus India hiring for software roles usually runs as a HirePro online assessment, then two technical interviews and a short HR round. Some off-campus paths skip the OA and go straight to virtual technical rounds. Expect strong emphasis on C/C++ fundamentals and Operating Systems rather than LeetCode-hard DSA.

## Online Assessment (OA)
- **Platform:** HirePro (often video-proctored); HackerRank appears in some drives.
- **Duration:** ~90 minutes total, commonly three timed sections of ~30 minutes each.
- **Sections & format:** (1) Aptitude / quant / logical reasoning (~20 MCQs); (2) Programming MCQs — C/C++ output prediction, pointers, structs/unions, OOPs; (3) Core CS — OS, DSA, CN/DBMS/OOPs (hardware/digital electives for some profiles).
- **Negative marking:** Commonly reported (~+1 / −0.25).
- **Topics tested:** Aptitude, C/C++ semantics, OS (threads, scheduling, memory), light DSA theory.
- **Difficulty:** Medium–Hard for MCQ density and negative marking; coding bar in interviews is Easy–Medium.

## Interview Rounds
- **Technical Round 1 (~45–60 min):** Live coding (linked lists dominate — Nth from end, cycle detect/length), OOPs (overload vs override, static, abstraction), pointer nuance.
- **Technical Round 2 (~1–2 hrs):** Deep OS — paging, page tables, scheduling, synchronization; resume/project grill; sometimes threading + architecture crossover questions.
- **HR (~10–15 min):** Relocation, team flexibility, why Qualcomm; often light after strong technical feedback.

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- OS (paging, scheduling, memory) is the most repeated interview theme across campus reports.
- Linked-list fluency and C pointer/memory questions matter more than grinding Hard graphs/DP.
- GFG Qualcomm archives are unusually predictive — questions often recur across batches.
- Discuss approach before coding; interviewers commonly give hints if you think aloud.
