## Process Overview
Typical India campus flow: HirePro (or WeCP for some intern drives) computer-based test → 1–2 technical interviews → HR. Embedded Software is commonly open to CSE/MnC; analog/digital roles skew ECE/EEE. Accuracy on the MCQ OA matters more than attempting every question.

## Online Assessment (OA)
- **Platform:** HirePro most common; WeCP reported for some intern drives.
- **Duration:** Section-timed — aptitude ~20–30 min; role technical ~30–45 min (variants ~75 min total for software-only MCQ sets).
- **Sections & format:** Aptitude (reasoning, probability, permutations, DI); technical MCQs for the applied role — C/C++/Java/Python output, OS, CN, DBMS/SQL, OOPs; embedded tracks add C-heavy and architecture questions. Many software drives are MCQ-only (no live coding in OA).
- **Negative marking:** Reported on some multi-section HirePro papers (~+1 / −0.25).
- **Topics tested:** C language semantics, DBMS/SQL, OS/COA basics, aptitude; role-specific electronics for non-software tracks.
- **Difficulty:** Medium; SQL/DBMS and C nuance often feel tougher than aptitude.

## Interview Rounds
- **Technical (often single long round, ~45–110 min):** Deep resume and project walkthrough; C mastery (pointers, memory, bit ops); 1–2 approachable DSA problems; for embedded — microprocessors, digital basics.
- **Second technical (when present):** More CS fundamentals or hardware depth depending on role.
- **HR:** Why TI, Bengaluru relocation, hobbies/open-source, joining timeline.

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- **C language mastery** beats heavy LeetCode grinding for TI software/embedded tracks.
- DBMS/SQL is a recurring weak spot — prepare joins, constraints, and “guess the result” style questions.
- Interviewers probe resume line-by-line; be ready for 45+ minutes on one project.
- Prefer accuracy over volume on HirePro; shortlists often prioritize correct answers.
