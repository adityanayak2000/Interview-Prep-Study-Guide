# Accenture
**Role(s):** Associate Software Engineer (ASE); Advanced ASE (AASE); Advanced Application Engineering Analyst / AEH (AAEA)  
**Tier:** G  
**Status:** done-v3

## Process Overview
Mainstream India campus hiring for ASE/AASE/AEH is a consistent **4-stage funnel**: (1) cognitive + technical MCQ, (2) coding assessment, (3) communication assessment, (4) combined technical + HR interview. Stages 1–2 are eliminatory; communication is mandatory but often treated as non-eliminatory. A separate Technology Development Program with a pitch-deck final round exists — do not mix it with this mainstream path.

## Online Assessment (OA)
- **Platform:** Unknown (vendor not named in primary accounts).
- **Round 1 — Cognitive + Technical MCQ:** ~90 questions / ~90 minutes across verbal, critical/logical, abstract reasoning, MS Office/common apps, pseudocode, and networking/security/cloud fundamentals (exact section counts can vary by drive).
- **Negative marking:** None (explicit in primary accounts).
- **Round 2 — Coding:** 2 problems / ~45 minutes, easy–medium; languages commonly include C/C++/Java/Python/.NET. How many problems you must fully clear to advance (and whether that affects ASE vs AASE/AEH) appears **batch-dependent** across sources.
- **Round 3 — Communication:** ~6 speaking/reading sections (~63 items; ~30 minutes of active test) with a multi-day completion window in some drives.
- **Difficulty:** Easy–medium overall.

## Interview Rounds
- **Single combined Technical + HR sitting** (often ~7–30 minutes): intro, heavy resume/project grilling, internship/hackathon/leadership probes, conflict/failure behavioral questions, then candidate Q&A.
- Live DSA/CS theory in Round 4 is **not** the heavy pattern in first-hand transcripts (those topics skew to OA pseudocode/networking and curated Q-banks).
- Dedicated HLD/LLD: not found for mainstream ASE/AASE/AEH.

## Topic Weights
| DSA | OS | C/C++ | DBMS | CN | LLD | HLD | Concurrency | Aptitude |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Med (OA coding) | Low (OA) | Low–Med (OA/pseudo) | Low | Low–Med (OA) | None | None | Low | High |

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- Clear the MCQ + coding gates cleanly; communication must still be completed.
- Interview prep priority: crisp project stories over Hard LeetCode.
- Know which offer tier (ASE vs AASE/AEH) your drive maps coding performance to — policy varies by batch.
- Keep TDP pitch-deck track separate if that is your path.
