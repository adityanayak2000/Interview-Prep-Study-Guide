## Process Overview
Zinnia (life/annuity insurtech; formerly SE2 — India hubs Noida/Pune/Hyderabad/Bengaluru) typically runs **~3 rounds**: coding OA → technical (± projects/SQL/OOP) → HR. Some older on-campus drives insert a Group Discussion after OA. Off-campus intern loops can be Technical → HR → CTO without a large OA.

_Caveat: medium confidence — OA duration/question count varies by drive (60–70+ min; 2–3 problems)._

## Online Assessment (OA)
- **Platform:** HackerRank named in at least one 2025 Bengaluru review; other drives unnamed.
- **Duration:** ~60 minutes (GFG on-campus) to ~70 minutes (Glassdoor) — not fixed.
- **Format:** Pure coding (no aptitude MCQ section reported). Examples: 2 problems (easy+medium or medium+hard); 3 medium problems in another Hyderabad report.
- **Negative marking:** Unknown.
- **Topics:** Arrays/strings (reverse, palindrome), two-sum variants, cyclic string similarity, co-prime/LCM array reduction (CodinzHub tag). Graphs/DP-heavy OA sets not the dominant pattern.
- **Difficulty:** Mostly easy–medium; occasional Hard-rated item.

## Interview Rounds
- **DSA:** Easy–medium array/string problems; trees/graphs/DP rarely reported in live rounds.
- **SQL/DBMS:** Repeated emphasis (joins and up); MongoDB vs SQL appears in at least one intern account.
- **OOP:** Abstraction / Java OOPs / C++ or Python OOP depending on interviewer.
- **Projects:** Consistently major.
- **HLD/LLD:** Not found for entry-level.
- **Insurance-domain actuarial content:** Not found in interview reports.
- **HR:** Why Zinnia (formerly SE2), fit, teamwork; one 2025 note mentioned AI trends — uncorroborated single data point.

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- Prioritize medium array/string DSA + SQL joins + OOP explanations.
- Process can be fast (same-day tech+HR on some campus batches).
- Do not assume insurance-domain quiz content for fresher SWE.
