## Process Overview
Netskope is a **security / SASE** company; India campus and intern pipelines emphasize **OS + CN + DSA**. Best round-by-round public detail is still **2019 campus** write-ups — treat as the prep pattern until a newer campus report appears. Company has continued India campus engagement (e.g. BITS Pilani Goa visit signals) and intern relaunch; **2024–2026 OA specs are largely undocumented**.

| Path | Entry | Typical flow |
|---|---|---|
| Campus / campus-direct (sourced 2019) | TPO | Online MCQ → Online coding → Tech 1 → Tech 2 → HR |
| Off-campus / experienced | Careers / LinkedIn | Recruiter → HM screen → DSA (± CoderPad) → design (senior+) → HM |
| Intern | Campus / LinkedIn | Separate intern funnel; less public round detail |

**Campus vs off-campus:** Campus historically used batch **HackerEarth** MCQ + coding; off-campus often starts with recruiter screens and may use **CoderPad** for live coding. Aptitude was absent in at least one campus-direct report. Senior loops add HLD.

**M.E./M.Tech CS:** GFG eligibility summary includes Bachelor’s or Master’s in CS/IT. Expect same fundamentals bar; no separate PG rubric found.

## Online Assessment (OA)
**Recent (2024–2026) campus OA format: Unknown** after search of GFG/Medium/LeetCode Discuss/Glassdoor/TheJobOverflow. Below is the **sourced 2019 campus pattern** only.

- **Platform:** **HackerEarth** (coding; MCQ platform named HackerEarth in primary reports)
- **Duration (2019 reports differ):** MCQ ~60–90 min; Coding ~75–90 min — follow your invite
- **Sections:** Often **two separate tests** — CS fundamentals MCQ, then DSA coding
- **Negative marking:** One report says **none**; others unknown
- **MCQ topics:** OS, CN, DBMS, DSA; sometimes Java/Python
- **Coding:** **2–3** problems; efficiency matters (naive approaches fail shortlist in reports)
- **Difficulty:** Medium; some optimized/harder coding sets reported
- **Aptitude:** Not a consistent theme for campus-direct

## Interview Rounds
### Campus model (2019 GFG / Manipal campus-direct)
1. **Online MCQ** — OS, CN, DBMS, DSA
2. **Online coding** — 2–3 DSA problems
3. **Technical 1** — 1–2 DSA (LL, intervals, puzzles); language fundamentals
4. **Technical 2** — DSA + **OS/CN** scenarios; mini-design (queues, elevators, overload)
5. **HR / managerial** — motivation, goals, communication

### Off-campus (entry-ish / experienced)
1. Recruiter / video screen — language basics + small programs
2. DSA rounds — strings, LL, top-K patterns
3. Project / systems — throughput, networking, product scenarios
4. Design + HM (senior+) — malware detection / API-DB style HLD reported at staff level

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- Prep **OS + CN as seriously as DSA** — security-company signal in OA and interviews.
- Linked lists, strings, intervals, grid/matrix problems recur; practice merge-intervals style.
- Scenario questions (queues, rate limiting, client backoff) appear in Tech 2.
- Communication matters in design/senior rounds — verbal engagement is scored.
- Confirm current OA with placement cell; public 2019 detail may be stale for format.
