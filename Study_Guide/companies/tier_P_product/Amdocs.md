## Process Overview
Amdocs India (telecom software; Pune / Gurugram / Mumbai) runs a **fundamentals-heavy** fresher loop: proctored OA → optional psychometric/GD → technical → HR. **M.Tech/ME are explicitly eligible** on multiple campus drives; same pipeline as B.Tech.

| Path | Entry | Typical flow |
|---|---|---|
| Campus ASE / SDE | TPO | PPT → OA (HirePro/HackerRank) → (± psychometric / GD) → Technical → HR |
| Off-campus | jobs.amdocs.com | Emailed OA → same fundamentals interviews |

**Campus vs off-campus:** Same OA + SQL/Unix/OOP bar; campus may add PPT recall in HR and occasional GD.

**M.E./M.Tech CS:** Documented M.Tech selects on campus drives; InterviewBit/ECED list ME/M.Tech. No BITS-specific write-up — process matches other tier campuses.

## Online Assessment (OA)
**Varies strongly by year/drive** — confirm invite.

- **Platform:** **HirePro** (classic) or **HackerRank** (recent); older LUGO
- **Duration:** ~90 min HirePro; HackerRank ASE/FTE reports **150–200 min**
- **Sections (variants):** Language output MCQs (C/C++/Java pick one) · coding (1–3) · SQL/MySQL · Linux/shell/OS/DBMS/OOP MCQs · aptitude/verbal; some drives integrity/personality round
- **Negative marking:** Unknown
- **Topics:** Easy–medium DSA (arrays, strings, LL, easy DP); SQL joins/aggregates; Unix commands
- **Difficulty:** Easy–medium coding; MCQ breadth is the volume challenge

## Interview Rounds
1. **Technical (45–90 min)** — resume/projects; live DSA (LL, BST, strings); **SQL**; C++/Java OOP; **Unix/Linux**; OS/DBMS theory; puzzles
2. **Psychometric** — often non-elimination (~15 min)
3. **GD** — some ASE drives
4. **HR (10–25 min)** — why Amdocs, location flexibility, higher studies

## Topic Weights
| DSA | OS | C/C++ | DBMS | CN | LLD | HLD | Concurrency | Aptitude |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| High | Med–High | High | High | Low–Med | Low | Negligible | Negligible | High |

*(SQL + Unix/Linux are very high in practice — tested in OA and interviews.)*

## Recurring Patterns & Tips
- Prep **SQL + Unix** as seriously as DSA — telecom-culture differentiator.
- Language chosen in OA often reappears as output questions in tech.
- Partial OA coding can still shortlist if MCQs are strong.
- Resume integrity — project deep-dives are common.
- System Analyst track may insist on Java or C++ only.
